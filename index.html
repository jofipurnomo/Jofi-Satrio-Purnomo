About Me

I have a background in chemistry and materials science, with research experience in the design and synthesis of functional materials and their physicochemical characterization. My previous work focused on developing adsorbent materials for textile wastewater treatment, which strengthened my interest in environmentally relevant material applications.

Currently, I am pursuing a master's degree in Japan in Materials Science and Engineering at University of Toyama, where my research focuses on photocatalytic systems for solar-to-chemical energy conversion. I am particularly interested in the rational design of molecular and hybrid photocatalysts for light-driven water splitting. My goal is to contribute to the development of efficient, sustainable energy materials through a deeper understanding of structure–function relationships in photocatalytic systems.
